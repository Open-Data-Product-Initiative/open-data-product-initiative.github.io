What is Open (Source) Data Product Specification?

The Open Data Product Specification (ODPS) is a transformative initiative hosted by the Linux Foundation since May 14th, 2024. It provides a vendor-neutral, fully computational, open-source, machine-readable YAML-based data product metadata model designed to reshape how organizations manage and monetize data.

Suitable for both open and commercial data products, ODPS offers 12 standardized data product pricing plans to cater to diverse business models. The "Open" represents the accessibility of the specification, enabling organizations to manage and monetize data effectively. Whether providing data for free or as a commercial offering, ODPS ensures flexible and scalable solutions to meet varied pricing strategies.

Standardized Data Quality, SLA, and Data Contracts support: This feature automates the processes of monitoring, compliance, and governance. It ensures that agreements on data structure, quality, and governance are consistently enforced, streamlining collaboration between data producers and consumers.

Computational Approach: Embracing an Everything-as-Code philosophy, ODPS facilitates automated workflows and programmable data management for Data Quality, SLA, and Pricing Plans. This model enhances scalability while optimizing operational efficiency. ODPS supports all major platforms such as Stripe, Checkout, PayPal, SodaCL, MonteCarlo, DQOps, and Prometheus.

12 Highlighted Features of ODPS

The Open Data Product Specification provides a comprehensive set of features that make it the ideal solution for managing and monetizing data products.

Vendor-neutral

ODPS is designed to work with any vendor or platform, allowing organizations to avoid vendor lock-in and maintain flexibility in their data infrastructure.

Machine-readable

The YAML-based format ensures that ODPS specifications can be easily processed by machines, enabling automation and integration with existing systems.

Related Use Cases

ODPS allows for the inclusion of real-world use cases, providing context and demonstrating the practical applications of data products.

Data Contracts Support

Support for Data Contract Specification (DCS) and Open Data Contract Specification (ODCS), ensuring clear agreements between data providers and consumers.

Extendable

The specification is designed to be easily extended to accommodate new features, requirements, and use cases as they emerge.

Localization Support

Built-in multilingual support allows data products to be described and presented in multiple languages, expanding their reach and accessibility.

Data Quality

Integration with data quality tools like SodaCL, MonteCarlo, and DQOps ensures that data products meet the highest standards of quality and reliability.

Computational

The Everything-as-Code approach enables automated workflows and programmable data management, enhancing efficiency and scalability.

Licensing Attributes

Comprehensive licensing information ensures clear understanding of usage rights and restrictions for data products.

Data Access

Detailed specifications for how data can be accessed, ensuring seamless integration with existing systems and workflows.

Related Products

Support for linking related data products, creating a network of interconnected resources that enhance value and utility.

Pricing Plans

Integration with payment platforms like Stripe, Checkout, and PayPal, facilitating monetization and subscription management for data products.

The History of ODPS

From its inception to becoming a Linux Foundation project

2022

February 1

ODPS 1.0 Released

The first MVP production version of the Open Data Product Specification was released, establishing the foundation for a standardized approach to data product management.

2023

April 1

ODPS 2.0 Released

Building on the success of the initial version, ODPS 2.0 introduced enhanced features and improved interoperability with existing data systems.

2023

August 22

ODPS 2.1 Released

This update refined the specification further, incorporating feedback from early adopters and expanding its capabilities.

2024

May 14

Joined Linux Foundation

A significant milestone as ODPS became a Linux Foundation project, demonstrating its importance to the open-source community and the data industry as a whole.

2024

May 22

ODPS 3.0 Released

The first release as a Linux Foundation project, featuring major enhancements to the specification's capabilities and interoperability.

2024

November 27

ODPS 3.1 Released

The latest production version, bringing enhanced data product capabilities, improved computational model features, and expanded support for data contracts and quality frameworks.
