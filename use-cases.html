ODPS Use Cases

Discover how organizations are using ODPS to transform their data strategies

Transforming Data Management and Monetization

The following use cases demonstrate how the Open Data Product Specification (ODPS) is being applied across various industries and scenarios. While these challenges can be addressed by vendor-specific and isolated solutions, ODPS provides a comprehensive, full-stack data product model that solves all these use cases in a single, unified package.

Explore how organizations are leveraging ODPS to break down data silos, enhance collaboration, increase transparency, and drive value from their data assets.

Increase Internal Transparency and Data Reuse

Many organizations struggle with internal data silos, limiting data sharing and collaboration. A Harvard Business Review study shows that companies treating data like a product reduce time to implement new use cases by up to 90% and decrease costs by up to 30%. ODPS breaks down these silos with a transparent, machine-readable metadata model, enhancing data discovery, comparison, and reuse across business units.

The Challenge

A global financial services company was struggling with data trapped in departmental silos. Teams were unaware of existing datasets created by other departments, leading to duplicated efforts, inconsistent analyses, and missed opportunities for collaboration.

The ODPS Solution

By implementing ODPS, the company created a standardized catalog of data products with rich metadata. Each data product included:

  • Comprehensive business-level descriptions with 120+ attributes
  • Detailed use case examples showing how data could be applied
  • Clear data access information with defined endpoints and protocols
  • Links to related data products, creating a network of connected resources

The Results

  • 58% reduction in time spent searching for data
  • 72% increase in cross-departmental data reuse
  • $3.2 million annual savings from eliminated duplicate efforts
  • 43% faster time-to-insight for new analytical projects

Boost Sales by Highlighting Data Value

Data monetization often fails because customers don't perceive value before purchasing. ODPS improves customer experience by detailing data quality, usage conditions, governance, and alternatives. It offers a comprehensive framework to convey the data product's value clearly, supporting informed purchasing decisions.

The Challenge

A leading market research company was struggling to effectively monetize its vast data assets. Potential customers were hesitant to purchase data products without clear understanding of the quality, scope, and usage rights, leading to lengthy sales cycles and low conversion rates.

The ODPS Solution

The company implemented ODPS to create comprehensive, transparent data product descriptions that clearly communicated value to potential customers:

  • Implemented 12 standardized pricing plans, including freemium options for value validation
  • Created a detailed data quality framework with 8 measurable indicators to build trust
  • Developed a comprehensive licensing model with 21 clearly defined conditions and rights
  • Published SLAs with specific performance guarantees and support commitments

The Results

  • 125% increase in data product sales
  • 67% reduction in sales cycle duration
  • 84% increase in freemium-to-paid conversions
  • 92% customer satisfaction rating for product transparency

Facilitate Data Product Team Collaboration

Transitioning to a data-as-a-product approach changes team structures and responsibilities, requiring cross-disciplinary collaboration. ODPS provides a standardized model to unify business, technical, marketing, security, and legal efforts, facilitating effective collaboration and reducing the need for custom solutions.

The Challenge

A multinational technology company was transitioning to a data mesh architecture with decentralized ownership, but struggled with inconsistent data product implementations and fragmented collaboration between domain teams, data engineers, and business stakeholders.

The ODPS Solution

The company adopted ODPS as a standardized framework for data product development, providing:

  • A unified model with attributes spanning licensing, pricing, technical specifications, and governance
  • Standardized marketing descriptions, value propositions, and documentation templates
  • Automated workflows through DataOps attributes and access definitions
  • Cross-functional dashboards showing product status across all dimensions

The Results

  • 79% improvement in cross-team collaboration ratings
  • 64% reduction in time-to-market for new data products
  • 45% fewer development iterations required
  • 88% decrease in cross-team coordination issues

Advance Open Data to the Next Level

Traditional metadata standards like DCAT are insufficient for the modern data marketplace. ODPS extends DCAT to support both open and commercial data, improving discoverability, quality, and monetization opportunities.

The Challenge

A municipal government had invested heavily in an open data portal but struggled with low usage rates, quality issues, and the inability to track impact. They wanted to commercialize premium versions of certain datasets to generate revenue while maintaining free access to basic versions.

The ODPS Solution

The municipality implemented ODPS to transform their open data portal:

  • Defined an 8-indicator data quality framework with automated monitoring and reporting
  • Implemented tiered pricing plans allowing both free access and premium options
  • Created detailed SLAs specifying uptime, refresh rates, and support levels
  • Enhanced metadata with use cases, beneficiaries, and documented success stories

The Results

  • 340% increase in data consumption
  • $450,000 annual revenue from premium data products
  • 23 new startups built on the municipal data
  • National recognition for open data innovation

Ensure Consistency with Linting

ODPS enables automated linting to maintain consistent "look and feel" across data products, improving maintenance and user experience. A machine-readable specification allows organizations to apply design guidelines effectively.

  • Early error detection
  • Compliance with design standards
  • Consistent user experience

Enrich API Access with Business Metadata

ODPS integrates business metadata into API specifications, providing a comprehensive view of data products, enhancing usability, and supporting multiple languages for broader audience reach.

  • Enhanced API documentation
  • Business context for technical assets
  • Multilingual support

Prototyping for Product-Market Fit

ODPS enables rapid prototyping and mockups, allowing businesses to test product-market fit before full development. It supports A/B testing and customer feedback gathering, minimizing waste and guiding development.

  • Rapid product view generation
  • Iterative testing and feedback
  • Market validation before development
